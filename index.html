Ryan Kim

Student Researcher — AI for Healthcare

Palatine, IL

ryankim17920@gmail.com

I’m a student researcher working at the intersection of AI and healthcare. I build ML systems that solve real problems in oncology, genomics, and medical imaging.

Currently, I’m a research intern at the AI in Medicine & Computational Biology Lab at Weill Cornell Medicine, investigating systematic bias in AI tumor purity estimators. I was also a Simons Summer Research Fellow at the Yurovsky Lab at Stony Brook University, where I developed DNABERT-based prognostic models for glioblastoma survival.

I’m the founder of Papers2Code.org, a platform indexing 300k+ ML/AI papers lacking public code, and a lead developer at MathLinks.org, a math competition platform serving 150k+ members.

Previously, I was a research intern at the Advanced Computing & Oncology Lab at Memorial Sloan Kettering, where I built DSPy-optimized LLM pipelines for extracting disease progression events from clinical reports.
